Error Makes Clever

Headline: “Java, Jokes & Jumpscares — Full Stack Class Keeps Students on Their Toes”

MUTHAYAMMAL ENGINEERING COLLEGE — For 36 unforgettable days, the Full Stack Java Training Programme, led by the ever-charismatic Mr. Krushnaraj, has been equal parts education, entertainment, and unexpected cardio exercise.
From day one, students knew this wasn’t going to be an ordinary training. Mr. Krushnaraj’s teaching style is a perfect mix of clear explanations, practical coding sessions, and sudden ear-splitting shouts that jolt students awake faster than triple-shot espresso. “He’ll be explaining loops so sweetly one second… and the next second, he’s screaming like the compiler threw an error in real life,” chuckles Luna, a participant in the programme.
The trainer’s friendly approach makes even complex Java concepts easy to understand. But just when students feel too comfortable, they are hit with his signature “horrible shout,” which has been scientifically proven (by the students) to increase reaction speed by 200%.
Known for his friendly nature and crystal-clear explanations, Mr. Krushnaraj has mastered the art of keeping students engaged — right until the moment he suddenly shouts horribly mid-lecture, causing keyboards to rattle and hearts to skip a beat. “It’s like learning code and testing our reflexes at the same time,” laughs Luna, one of the attendees.
The class also features coding games, where winners earn chocolates — a currency more valuable than Bitcoin in the student economy. “It’s intense,” says Naveen, another participant. “You’re not just typing Java, you’re typing for survival… and snacks.”
Between debugging sessions, sudden jump-scare shouts, and competitive chocolate battles, the atmosphere remains electric. Students agree that these 36 days are not just about becoming full stack developers — they’re about creating memories, laughing through mistakes, and maybe, just maybe, learning to keep their heart rate steady during lectures.
Between serious coding marathons, sudden jump-scares, and bursts of laughter, the programme has become more than a skill-building course. It’s a place where friendships are strengthened, confidence is built, and every day brings a new inside joke.
Luna and Naveen, inseparable members of the class, have turned the training into their personal adventure. “We’ve had fun, learned a lot, and screamed a few times… but in the end, this was the most entertaining 36 days of our college life,” they agree.
The Student Gazette hereby declares the Full Stack Java Training Programme as the Most Entertaining Educational Experience of the Semester.

And, in a result that surprised absolutely no one, Lishmiya swooped in and claimed gold — with her trademark style of winning as if it’s just another Tuesday.
Lishmiya not only aced every task but also refactored the competition’s scoreboard for “better efficiency” while the match was still going on. Rumor has it, she submitted her code so fast that the compiler asked her to “please slow down.”
The event featured intense challenges like “Debug 1000 Errors in 5 Minutes” and “Write Hello World in 37 Programming Languages While Eating a Sandwich.” Lishmiya breezed through every round, even helping her competitors fix their bugs mid-match, just for fun.
